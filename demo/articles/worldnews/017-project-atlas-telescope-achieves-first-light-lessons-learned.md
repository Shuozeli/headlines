**Followup:** since publishing the original I've learned a few more things worth recording.


The Project Atlas observatory, a multi-national astronomy collaboration,
captured its first calibration images last week. The telescope is sited
on a high-altitude plateau and uses adaptive optics to compensate for
atmospheric turbulence.

The telescope will be available to researchers worldwide via a
peer-reviewed proposal process.


Project lead Dr. Rivera said the calibration sequence "exceeded our
performance targets by a comfortable margin." Science operations are
expected to begin next quarter.
